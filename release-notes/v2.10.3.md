# Atelier 2.10.3 — plan meters and a real provider switcher

- Your Claude and Codex plan limits are now visible in Atelier — the same meters claude.ai and the ChatGPT app show. A compact gauge next to the composer's context meter tracks your session window and weekly usage, and the session-config popover shows every window in full: percentage bar, severity, and reset time.
- Claude limits come from the CLI's own zero-cost usage surface — refreshed at session start, after every turn, and whenever the stream pushes a rate-limit change. Model-scoped weekly limits (e.g. Fable) are included.
- Codex limits come from the app-server's rate-limit surface with live push updates, including plan type.
- Switching between Claude and Codex is now a proper segmented switcher at the top of the session-config popover — both providers always visible, the selected one slides between them, in Atelier's sharp-cornered visual language. Replaces the buried text link, and the popover no longer changes size when you switch.
- A provider that isn't installed, or a switch attempted mid-turn, now dims with an explanatory tooltip instead of silently doing nothing.
