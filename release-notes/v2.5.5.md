# Atelier 2.5.5 — snappier tabs, calmer memory, no more paste glitches

- **Tab switching is instant.** Switching used to rebuild the entire chat
  view from scratch — every message, recreated. Now the view updates in
  place, so switching between conversations is immediate even in long chats.
- **Long chats scroll like new ones.** A second pass on the scroll engine:
  only the most recent messages are laid out at once (older ones expand on
  demand with "show earlier"), so a month-old conversation no longer scrolls
  worse than a fresh tab.
- **The composer stopped stealing focus.** Clicking into the transcript or a
  co-driven terminal while a reply was streaming used to get yanked back to
  the input box — sometimes every fraction of a second. Fixed at the root.
- **Pasting long text no longer glitches the app.** A paste was quietly
  triggering a chain of expensive work — this is now done once, correctly,
  instead of dozens of times per second.
- **Idle tabs stop costing memory.** A background tab left alone for 10
  minutes now rests quietly (shown as "Resting") instead of holding half a
  gigabyte for nothing — it wakes instantly the moment you reply.
