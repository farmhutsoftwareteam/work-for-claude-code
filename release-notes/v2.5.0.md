# Atelier 2.5.0 — your projects, recognizable at a glance

Project logos land on tabs and the rail, and the co-driven terminal learns
to fold out of your way while it works.

## Project favicons

- **Tabs and the project rail now show each project's own logo** — discovered
  from canonical locations only (`public/favicon.*`, Next's `app/favicon.ico`,
  Expo's `assets/icon.png`, apple-touch-icon, …), never a fuzzy search.
- **Quality-gated so nothing ugly ever shows**: a logo must decode, be roughly
  square, sanely sized, and under 1MB — then it's downscaled once to a crisp
  thumbnail. Fails any check → the clean dot you already know.
- **Status still wins**: while a session works, the radar ring wraps the logo;
  when a tab needs you, the clay glow pulses behind it; the rail's live dot
  outranks branding.

## Fold the terminal while it works

- The co-driven terminal now **collapses to its slim header** (chevron, or
  click the header): the command and live timer stay, the wall of output gets
  out of your way.
- While folded, a **one-line tail of the latest output** ticks along so you
  always know what it's doing — and when the command finishes, the header
  flips **✓ green or ✗ red** with the total duration. A folded terminal still
  reports its outcome.
- **Secure prompts force the pane open** — a password needs your keyboard, so
  it's never allowed to hide.
